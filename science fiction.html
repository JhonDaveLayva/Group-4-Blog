SCIENCE FICTION

March 14, 2023 / 21st Century Literary Genre
Olayon, Dana Tango, Cyrus Layva, Jhon Dave

Welcome to our informative blog about science fiction, a genre of literature that has captured the imagination of readers and viewers for decades. Science fiction is characterized by imaginative and often speculative stories that explore the possibilities of technology, alternate realities, and extraterrestrial life.

Science fiction, abbreviated "sci-fi," is a type of fiction that incorporates real or imagined science and technology as part of the storyline, setting, or subject. The "fiction" portion of science fiction implies that it is a fictitious narrative, not a true report. The term science refers to the fact that the narrative incorporates science or technology, no matter how developed, that is presented as being founded on true scientific principles rather than incorporating magic or the supernatural. Yet, science fiction is not necessarily frightening, and most sci-fi novels incorporate aspects of other genres such as mystery, romance, humor, and fantasy.

HERE ARE SOME EXAMPLES OF SCIENCE FICTIONS:

DR. STONE

by Riichiro Inagaki

Several thousand years after a mysterious phenomenon petrified all of humanity, a brilliant, scientifically-minded teen wakes up in this new world alone. Vowing to bring back all of civilization, Senku sets out to revive all 7 billion people on Earth. With millions of years of human development to rebuild and countless challenges to be faced, will Senku and his friends succeed in reviving everyone, or is their fate set in stone?

FAHRENHEIT 451

by Ray Bradbury

Fahrenheit 451 is a dystopian novel where books are banned and burned by "firemen." Guy Montag, a fireman, becomes disillusioned and starts secretly collecting and reading books. He is targeted by the government and must flee. The novel explores themes of censorship, technology, and the power of knowledge. It ends with Montag joining a group of rebels dedicated to preserving literature and freedom of thought.

DUNE

by Frank Herbert

Dune is a science fiction novel about the nobleman Paul Atreides who becomes embroiled in a conflict for control of the desert planet Arrakis, the only source of the valuable spice melange. As Paul becomes more involved with the planet's indigenous people, the Fremen, he discovers that he may be their messiah figure. The novel explores themes of politics, religion, ecology, and power.

MATRIX

by Lana Wachowski

The Matrix is a teenage boy's dream. There's action, fighting, cutting-edge special effects, murderous robots, evil authority figures, an overriding pseudo-conspiracy theory and, most wonderful of all, an ineloquent social outcast who eventually becomes a flying kung fu Jesus.
