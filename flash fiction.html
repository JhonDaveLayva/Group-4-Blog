SCIENCE FICTION

March 14, 2023 / 21st Century Literary Genre
Masuhud, Melhana Calumpang, Kristian De Jesus, John Benedict

DEFINITION OF FASH FICTION

Fiction of a type characterized by being very short, typically consisting of only a few hundred words.

WHAT IS FLASH FICTION?

Short story that is typically under 1,500~ words. Very small flash fictions (under 75~ words) are called micro fictions.

The main elements of a flash fiction are the length, the character, and a bit of a twist at the end. The whole point of a flash fiction is that it’s short, very short. Flash fiction can be as short as a sentence if you want. Since this type of fiction is really short, the characterization will really be developed through very concrete imagery of that character’s perspective.

THE ELEMENTS OF FLASH FICTION

  • Emotion

    • What do you want your story to make your reader feel?

  • Character

    •Who is your story about?

  • Imagery

    •What strong, iconic imagery will your story use?

  • Inciting Incident

    •where will you start your story? As with all fiction, start late and end early. Start in the middle of your story. Maybe show something strange your character is doing to spark interest.

  • Hook Ending

    •What will your twist be?

HERE ARE SOME EXAMPLES OF SCIENCE FICTIONS:

BABY DOLLS

by Riichiro Inagaki Word count: 175 words

In an impressive small selection of expertly curated text, the narrator tells the story of how a teenage mother gave birth to her during a basement party in a bathtub with a moldy curtain. If you want to get excited about writing today, look no further.

HOUSEWIFE

by Amy Hempel Word count: 43 words

The quintessence of microfiction, this one sentence sums up the experience of a boring but decidedly ambitious housewife.

ANGELS AND BLUEBERRIES

by Tara Campbell Word count: 744 words

Campbell’s sweet, wholesome story posits various explanations for the color of the sky, one of which (naturally) involves angels and blueberries. Besides making you crave a fruit smoothie, it’ll open your eyes to the delightful possibilities of imagination when we disregard science for a few minutes.

AS THE NORTH WIND HOWLED

by Yu Hua Word count: 1, 371 words

Translated from the original Chinese, this brilliantly bizarre story follows a man who awakens one morning to find a stranger pounding down his door. The stranger insists that he’s come to visit his sick friend — the only trouble is, our narrator has no idea who he’s talking about. The uncanniness escalates from there, culminating in a dark yet comic ending that deftly comments on the oppressive nature of social obligations.
